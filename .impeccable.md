## Design Context

### Users
- Primary audience: investors.
- Primary job: evaluate the thesis quickly and decide whether to initiate an enquiry.
- Primary conversion goal: investor enquiry.

### Brand Personality
- Target tone: clinical, premium, ultra-trustworthy (calm, precise).
- Emotional goal: confidence and clarity without hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark-first, editorial + clinical minimalism: typography-led hierarchy, restrained accent usage, no decorative neon/glow.
- Avoid: “AI vibe” (neon/glow/cyber gradients), generic SaaS landing patterns, and endless templated card grids.
- Proof posture: remove impact numbers from the homepage; keep metrics on `Hospital Corner` only.

### Design Principles
1. Typography leads; surfaces support (not the other way around).
2. Calm motion only: state/feedback > decoration; no glow, no hype.
3. Reduce cognitive load: fewer repeated patterns, fewer containers, clearer CTAs.
4. Proof is scoped and intentional: metrics live where they’re evaluated (hospital business case), not on the investor story.
5. Consistency earns trust: spacing rhythm, copy voice, and interaction states stay coherent across routes.

## Design Context

### Users
- Hospital executives, ICU leadership, and clinical operations decision-makers evaluating digital care platforms.
- Clinicians and care teams who need confidence that the product supports real-time, secure, mobile visibility into patient data.
- Investors and strategic partners assessing commercialization readiness and outcomes claims.

### Brand Personality
- Calm, clinical, credible.
- Modern, assured, systems-level.
- Human-centered without being sentimental.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark, cinematic, high-contrast “night shift” tone with restrained glassmorphism used as a functional surface treatment (not decoration).
- Typography-led hierarchy (Instrument Serif for display, Inter for body), minimal ornamentation.
- Avoid loud gradients, neon accents, decorative blobs, and over-templated “SaaS hero” patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize clarity over cleverness (content and hierarchy should read in a fast skim).
2. Use tokens for color and spacing; avoid hard-coded UI color drift.
3. Motion should be subtle, purposeful, and respect reduced-motion preferences.
4. Accessibility is non-negotiable (keyboard, focus, contrast, semantics).
5. Performance is part of credibility (no jank, heavy effects, or unnecessary JS).
